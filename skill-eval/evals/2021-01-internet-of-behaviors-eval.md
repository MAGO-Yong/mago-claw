## What Was Done Well

1. **Topic alignment section is excellent**: Clearly defines IoB, distinguishes it from IoT and adjacent concepts, explains what it is NOT—this helps a non-expert quickly calibrate understanding
2. **Concrete industry examples**: Amazon Go, Progressive Snapshot, Disney MagicBand—these make abstract technology tangible
3. **Ethical dimension was properly treated**: Not just mentioned but given a full Round 2 deep-dive; the privacy spectrum and decision framework are genuinely useful
4. **Round 2 narrowing was logical**: Privacy-ethics was correctly identified as the most consequential unresolved question from Round 1
5. **Failure cases included**: Clearview AI, Cambridge Analytica—these ground the ethical risks in reality

## What Was Done Poorly

1. **Market size figures are approximate**: "~$2.1 billion" for behavioral analytics lacks a clear source attribution; MarketsandMarkets numbers are paywalled—could note uncertainty more explicitly
2. **Hype Cycle position assessment lacks basis**: Stating "Peak of Inflated Expectations heading toward Trough" without citing specific Gartner Hype Cycle data is a judgment call presented as fact
3. **"2022" IoB appeared as separate trend in that year's list**: The report doesn't note that IoB did NOT reappear in subsequent Gartner lists—what happened to the trend?
4. **Round 2 lacks new primary research**: The privacy framework section draws on general knowledge rather than specific 2021-era enterprise case studies with quantitative outcomes
5. **China's social credit system**: Mentioned but treated superficially—could be a more nuanced discussion of what's fact vs. media exaggeration

## Improvement Suggestions

1. Be explicit when market size figures come from secondary aggregator sources vs. primary research; flag uncertainty ranges
2. Cross-reference with subsequent Gartner Hype Cycles to note if/how the trend evolved after its initial designation
3. Round 2 should include at least one quantitative outcome from a real enterprise IoB program (ROI, adoption rate, etc.)
4. Add a "Who Should Care" section—which industries/roles are most relevant readers for this research
