## What Was Done Well

1. **Four-dimension framework clearly explained**: CX, EX, UX, MX distinctions are crisp; non-expert can immediately understand the taxonomy
2. **"What TX is NOT" section**: Prevents common misunderstandings, adds clarity
3. **Honest about trend longevity**: Noted that the "TX" label faded by 2024 even though the concept matured—this is valuable context
4. **Round 2 organizational analysis**: The ownership vacuum problem is the genuinely hard part, and the four structural models are practical
5. **USAA example**: Classic but appropriate reference with causal explanation

## What Was Done Poorly

1. **Market size figures lack rigor**: Numbers like "$10.5 billion" and "$32 billion" are cited to Mordor Intelligence without dates or methodology—these figures are rough and should be flagged as such
2. **Thin on failure cases**: No specific TX program that failed is cited—would strengthen the risk analysis
3. **Round 2 is somewhat thin**: The "recommendation framework" is sensible but generic; doesn't cite specific enterprises that used these models with outcomes
4. **No web search results incorporated**: The research appears to draw heavily on training knowledge without fresh web-sourced verification for 2024-2025 developments

## Improvement Suggestions

1. Search for and cite a specific TX failure case with analysis of why it failed
2. Include a 2024/2025 update on how TX has evolved—is it still being discussed or has it been superseded?
3. Market size figures should either be properly sourced (with date, methodology) or removed
4. Add a "Practical Entry Points" subsection—which technology investments give enterprises the fastest TX lift?
