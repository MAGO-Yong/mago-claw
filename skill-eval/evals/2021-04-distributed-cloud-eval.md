## What Was Done Well

1. **Key distinction from hybrid cloud**: The "who manages it" distinction is the essential point and it's explained clearly upfront
2. **Product mapping to hyperscalers**: AWS Outposts, Google Distributed Cloud, Azure Stack/Arc—concrete product names make this actionable
3. **Decision criteria box**: Simple yes/no questions help readers determine if distributed cloud applies to them
4. **Regulatory landscape in R2**: GDPR, PIPL, DPDP—multi-jurisdiction analysis is genuinely useful and shows research depth

## What Was Done Poorly

1. **Deutsche Bank case**: Referenced without quantitative outcomes—how much did it cost? What did they achieve?
2. **5G edge section could be deeper**: Telecommunications is the fastest-growing use case but gets one paragraph
3. **R2 is somewhat thin**: The data sovereignty deep-dive is good but shorter than ideal; could explore more specific enterprise case studies with outcomes
4. **Market size missing**: No estimate of distributed cloud market size or growth trajectory—this is a reportable figure from IDC/Gartner

## Improvement Suggestions

1. Add distributed cloud market size/growth figure from IDC or Synergy Research
2. Expand 5G/telecom edge computing as a use case—it's the most technically innovative
3. Add a "failure modes" section—when does distributed cloud fail to meet its promises?
4. R2 should include a comparison of how different hyperscalers handle the management plane sovereignty question
