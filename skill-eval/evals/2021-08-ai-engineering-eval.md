## What Was Done Well

1. **Central statistic drives the narrative**: "53% reach production" → "47% fail" → "AI engineering is why" is a clear logical arc
2. **R2 root cause taxonomy**: Four failure categories with percentages is excellent analytical content
3. **Industry examples are exceptional**: Netflix $1B value from personalization, Uber Michelangelo, Airbnb Bighead—these are industry-standard references that show depth
4. **Investment priority list**: Numbered 1-5 priority gives readers clear action guidance
5. **Feature store explanation**: Clear, non-obvious component that most reports would skip

## What Was Done Poorly

1. **DataOps/ModelOps/DevOps triad needs examples**: The definitions are okay but each needs a concrete "what this looks like in practice" sentence
2. **Market size estimates are rough**: "$1.9 billion MLOps market" without a specific source is an approximation
3. **The "Google internal research" in R2**: "4x more models deployed" cited to "Google internal research (2021)" without specific publication details—this weakens credibility

## Improvement Suggestions

1. Add a concrete "Day in the life of a model without MLOps vs. with MLOps" contrast
2. Verify Google research citation with specific paper or blog post reference
3. Add a "What AI Engineering Is NOT" to distinguish from data engineering and data science roles
