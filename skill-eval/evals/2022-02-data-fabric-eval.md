## What Was Done Well

1. **Data Fabric vs. Data Mesh clarification**: This debate confused the industry for years—the report handles it diplomatically and correctly
2. **Microsoft Fabric R2**: Timely, specific, the most practically useful section; 20,000 tenants in first year is a compelling adoption signal
3. **Comparison table in R2**: Microsoft vs. established vendors across 6 criteria—exactly what a reader needs to make a decision
4. **Five-layer architecture**: Makes an abstract concept architectural and concrete
5. **"80% time on data prep" statistic**: Vivid problem statement

## What Was Done Poorly

1. **Gartner claims "70% reduction"**: This is an unverified vendor/analyst claim that deserves skepticism—not presented as uncertain
2. **No specific case study with outcomes**: The financial services, healthcare, retail examples are vague—no specific organization cited
3. **Technical depth may alienate executives**: The architecture section is for technical readers; less accessible to business stakeholders

## Improvement Suggestions

1. Add a brief "Executive Summary" box at the top for non-technical readers
2. Verify and qualify the "70% effort reduction" claim with source methodology
3. Include at least one named organization as a data fabric case study with outcome
