## What Was Done Well

1. **Evolution narrative**: "Anywhere operations was crisis response; distributed enterprise is strategic model"—this is the key insight and it's clearly stated
2. **GitLab case study**: Specific, verifiable, with clear practices—not just "GitLab is distributed" but how they do it
3. **Innovation finding**: "More incremental, fewer breakthrough" is a nuanced empirical claim that adds depth
4. **Four business model sectors**: Retail, healthcare, education, professional services—shows breadth of applicability

## What Was Done Poorly

1. **Return-to-office tension underexplored**: 2023-2024 saw major corporate RTO mandates (Amazon, JPMorgan, Google)—this tension deserves more attention
2. **Quantitative outcomes for distributed enterprise ROI**: "20-40% office footprint reduction" is vague
3. **Middle management challenge missing**: Distributed enterprise is especially hard for middle managers who traditionally supervised presence

## Improvement Suggestions

1. Address the 2023-2024 RTO backlash explicitly—what drove it, what does the research say?
2. Quantify the real estate savings with specific company examples
3. Add a "Middle Management Transformation" section
