## What Was Done Well

1. **Hype cycle narrative**: 2021 peak → 2022 feature → 2022-2023 collapse—perfectly told with specific data points
2. **"What worked vs. what didn't work" structure**: Most useful organizing principle in any report; immediate clarity
3. **BMW digital twin $9B savings**: Vivid, specific, large-scale outcome
4. **"Metaverse as a term is largely discredited" conclusion**: Intellectually honest; consistent with actual industry trajectory
5. **Apple Vision Pro as inflection signal**: Correctly identifies the spatial computing pivot as the signal for the future direction

## What Was Done Poorly

1. **"$9 billion saved" for BMW**: Very large claim; should note this is over an undisclosed period and the methodology is unclear
2. **Roblox and Fortnite as "closest to functioning metaverse"**: Could develop this more—what would it take for gaming platforms to become the enterprise metaverse?
3. **Horizon Worlds user numbers may be outdated**: Meta updated their MAU reporting; this section could be verified

## Improvement Suggestions

1. Verify and contextualize the BMW $9B savings claim with more detail
2. Add a "Lessons for Future Trend Evaluation" section—the metaverse hype cycle is instructive for evaluating all emerging trends
3. Update Horizon Worlds statistics if more recent data is available
