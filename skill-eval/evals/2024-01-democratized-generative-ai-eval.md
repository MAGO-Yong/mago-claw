## What Was Done Well

1. **Before/after democratization contrast**: Clear, specific, changes the reader's mental model
2. **Shadow AI framing**: "40% of AI data breaches from shadow AI" is a compelling, specific risk
3. **5-layer governance framework**: Policy → Technical → Monitoring → People → Governance Body—comprehensive
4. **Enterprise vs. consumer tool comparison**: Pricing and features are specific and actionable
5. **"Channeling, not blocking"**: The final recommendation is the right policy advice

## What Was Done Poorly

1. **McKinsey $4.4T productivity estimate**: This is widely cited but based on rough assumptions; should be flagged as directional
2. **"60% Fortune 500 adopted Copilot"**: This is a Microsoft claim that should be noted as such with appropriate skepticism
3. **Open-source model governance**: Llama, Mistral self-hosting is an enterprise option not covered in governance framework

## Improvement Suggestions

1. Add open-source model deployment to the governance framework (separate concerns apply)
2. Flag Microsoft's self-reported adoption claims as requiring independent verification
3. Add a "Quick Start" governance guide for organizations just beginning—where to start in 30 days
