## What Was Done Well

1. **"AI assistant vs. AI monitoring" framing conclusion**: The most practically important insight in the report—absolutely correct and actionable
2. **Lockheed Martin 90% quality improvement**: Extraordinary outcome that demonstrates maximum ACWF value
3. **Knowledge capture section**: The baby boomer retirement wave is an underappreciated driver that gets appropriate attention
4. **Five-step coaching loop**: Clear process description that demystifies what AI coaching does
5. **Adoption barriers section**: Explicitly listing union considerations and worker resistance is realistic and important

## What Was Done Poorly

1. **Consumer driving coaching (IoB overlap)**: The telematics example feels out of place—it's a consumer application of a different trend (IoB)
2. **Cost of ACWF implementation**: No estimate of what AR-equipped warehouse costs vs. traditional operation
3. **COVID as driver**: The healthcare nursing shortage was significantly pandemic-driven; this context is briefly noted but could be more developed

## Improvement Suggestions

1. Remove the telematics example or explicitly connect it to ACWF rather than treating it as part of the workforce application
2. Add cost framework for AR/connected worker implementation (hardware, software, training, change management)
3. Include more on knowledge capture technology—the AI that extracts and structures expert knowledge is underexplored
