## What Was Done Well

1. **Spectrum of machine customers**: From Amazon Dash to HFT—the full range helps readers calibrate
2. **"Sellers who win machine customer market" section**: Specific criteria (best API, objective criteria, machine-readable catalog)—actionable for B2B strategy
3. **Legal principal-agent framework**: Clearly explains the current legal model without overstating what's resolved
4. **Tesla service scheduling**: Perfect example—not hypothetical, currently deployed
5. **Machine customer registry concept**: First-mover governance infrastructure thinking is valuable

## What Was Done Poorly

1. **"$11 trillion" Gartner estimate**: Enormous, questionable prediction not subjected to any scrutiny
2. **Legal section is thin**: The most novel section deserves more depth—specific jurisdiction differences, emerging case law
3. **Who are the early machine customer technology vendors?**: No company is specifically building "machine customer platforms"—this omission is notable

## Improvement Suggestions

1. Challenge the $11 trillion Gartner estimate with some methodology analysis—how did they arrive at this?
2. Search for actual machine customer platform vendors or standards bodies working on this
3. Add a section on "Machine Customer Protocols"—what technical standards would enable interoperability?
