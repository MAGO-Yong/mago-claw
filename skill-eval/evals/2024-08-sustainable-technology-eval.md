## What Was Done Well

1. **Google's self-reported sustainability failure**: "Goals may not be achievable" from Google's own report—most impactful data point in any sustainability report
2. **Training vs. inference distinction**: Important for readers to understand the different energy challenges
3. **Four AI energy solutions (model efficiency, hardware, carbon-aware, lifecycle)**: Comprehensive and actionable
4. **Quantization/pruning/distillation**: Specific, named efficiency techniques readers can investigate
5. **IEA 200 TWh projection**: Authoritative, specific, and alarming—exactly what decision-makers need

## What Was Done Poorly

1. **"Netherlands" comparison for data centers**: The 200 TWh/year = Netherlands electricity is a useful analogy but should clarify this is projected 2026 consumption, not current
2. **Carbon-aware computing tools are listed but not described**: WattTime and ElectricityMaps deserve a sentence on how they work
3. **No specific enterprise case study**: Every enterprise should be asking "how much energy does our AI use?"—a case study of a company that measured this would be valuable

## Improvement Suggestions

1. Add a calculation guide: "How to estimate your organization's AI energy consumption"
2. Develop carbon-aware computing explanation—scheduling batch AI in low-carbon hours is underutilized
3. Add a 2025/2026 outlook on AI chip efficiency improvements—the trajectory matters
