## Round 1: Foundational Research Memo

### 1. Overview

Total Experience (TX) appeared in both 2021 and 2022 Gartner strategic technology trends. For full foundational treatment, see `2021-02-total-experience.md`. This report focuses on 2022-specific developments and evidence.

**2022 Framing Change:** The 2022 version of TX had an additional dimension—the distributed enterprise context. As remote and hybrid work became permanent, connecting the customer experience (what customers experience) with the employee experience (what remote employees experience) became even more critical. Employees serving customers from home needed better tools to deliver excellent customer service.

**Gartner 2022 Prediction:** By 2023, 75% of organizations that exploit distributed enterprise benefits will realize revenue growth 25% faster than competitors.

### 2. 2022-Specific Developments

#### 2.1 TX Technology Maturation

By 2022, the key technology enablers of Total Experience had matured significantly:

**Digital Experience Platforms (DXPs):**
- **Sitecore**: Unified customer and employee experience platform; acquired several companies to complete DXP portfolio
- **Adobe Experience Platform**: Real-time customer data platform enabling TX personalization at scale
- **Salesforce Customer 360**: Unified view connecting customer service, marketing, commerce, and IT
- **ServiceNow**: Expanded from ITSM to employee experience platform (Now Platform for employee workflows)

**Experience Management Platforms:**
- **Qualtrics**: IPO'd 2021; expanded from NPS surveys to full experience management (XM) across customer and employee domains
- **Medallia**: Enterprise experience management; $2.4B valuation; went private via Thoma Bravo 2021

**Unified Communications Evolution:**
- Microsoft Viva (employee experience): Launched 2021; expanded 2022 to cover insights, learning, topics, connections—explicitly bridging EX and productivity in Teams
- The "Teams as TX platform" thesis: Microsoft positioned Teams as the center of TX for M365 customers

#### 2.2 Employee-Customer Connection Evidence

More evidence emerged in 2022 supporting the EX→CX connection:
- **Gallup State of the Workplace 2022**: Organizations with high employee engagement scored 23% higher profitability and 10% higher customer metrics
- **Temkin Group research**: "Companies with engaged employees have 81% higher customer satisfaction than those with disengaged employees"
- **Qualtrics research**: Employee experience programs correlated with 10-point improvement in net promoter scores when combined with CX programs

#### 2.3 Return to Office and TX

The 2022 RTO (Return to Office) push created new TX complexity:
- Employees expected remote-quality digital tools whether in-office or remote
- Hybrid meeting room technology became critical (equity for in-room vs. remote attendees)
- Wayfinding apps, desk booking systems, building management tools all needed to be as seamless as consumer apps
- Companies like Cisco (Webex Devices), Microsoft (Teams Rooms), Google (Meet hardware) invested heavily in hybrid meeting experiences

The hybrid work TX challenge: Same quality experience whether you're in the office or at home. Most organizations failed at this in 2022.

#### 2.4 Industry Adoption Examples

**Southwest Airlines TX Program:**
Southwest explicitly aligned customer satisfaction investments with employee experience investments—recognizing that "the happiness of employees directly impacts passenger experience." Launched enhanced digital tools for gate agents simultaneously with customer app improvements.

**USAA (Financial Services):**
USAA consistently ranks highest in both employee satisfaction and customer satisfaction surveys. Their "employee-first" culture explicitly treats employee experience as prerequisite to customer experience—a TX in practice before the concept was named.

**Airbnb:**
Post-COVID, Airbnb redesigned for "live and work from anywhere"—hosts, guests, and employees all interact with digital platforms. The TX challenge: maintaining quality experience across host-guest-employee triangle. Their 2022 product releases addressed host tools and guest experience simultaneously.

#### 2.5 Measurement of TX Success

By 2022, more sophisticated TX measurement frameworks emerged:
- **Combined XM index**: Tracking NPS (customer) and eNPS (employee) together with correlation analysis
- **Employee-to-Customer correlation dashboards**: Tools like Qualtrics showing where employee experience directly impacts customer metrics
- **Omnichannel experience scoring**: Measuring consistency across physical/digital touchpoints

## Round 2: Deep-Dive — The Hybrid Meeting Experience Problem

### Research Question

**Most pressing 2022 TX challenge:** Hybrid work created an immediate TX failure case: hybrid meetings where in-room participants have an excellent experience and remote participants have a poor one, or vice versa. What are enterprises doing to solve this?

### Deep Findings

#### The Hybrid Meeting Equity Problem

Research from Microsoft's Work Trend Index 2022:
- 43% of remote employees felt excluded in hybrid meetings
- Remote attendees 2x as likely to "drop off" mentally from meetings
- Physical meeting rooms configured for in-room participants (whiteboards only visible to room participants, cameras focused on presenter, audio designed for room)

This is a TX failure: excellent employee experience for in-room participants, poor experience for remote participants.

#### Technology Solutions

**Intelligent Camera Systems:**
- Microsoft Surface Hub + AI-powered camera (tracks speaker, zooms automatically)
- Poly Studio with "Director AI" (automatically finds and frames active speakers)
- Cisco Webex Board with multi-camera views

**Audio Technology:**
- Shure Stem ecosystem (distributed microphone arrays capture room audio without traditional mic management)
- Jabra PanaCast (180° room camera + intelligent audio)

**Software Solutions:**
- Microsoft Teams Premium (intelligent meeting recap, live translation, chapter summaries)
- Zoom Smart Gallery (virtual layout showing each in-room participant in separate video tile, equalizing with remote participants)

**Room Design:**
- "Equity-focused" room design: smaller rooms with better cameras rather than large conference rooms; everyone on same video call even if adjacent

#### Outcome Data

- Organizations implementing equity-focused hybrid meeting rooms: 30% reduction in reported remote participant exclusion (Microsoft research)
- Video conferencing equipment market grew 25% 2021-2022 as enterprises upgraded for hybrid
- "Hybrid meeting room upgrades" became $5-10K per room investment at scale

### Round 2 Conclusion

Hybrid meeting equity is the immediate, solvable TX problem of 2022. The technology is available and ROI is clear (employee satisfaction, meeting effectiveness). Organizations investing in hybrid meeting rooms—smaller rooms with better cameras, intelligent audio, software equalization—are delivering TX in one of the most everyday touchpoints. This is where TX philosophy meets practical, measurable implementation.
